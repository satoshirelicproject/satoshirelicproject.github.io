Project Snapshot

Launch Order: Memecoin first, then the broader research ecosystem.

Scope: Early Satoshi-era wallets only.

Goal: Fund development, build community, and launch transparent coordination tools.

Project Summary

SatoshiRelic begins as a memecoin and grows into a broader research and coordination ecosystem.

The token is the first public layer of the project and helps fund the development of the full system.

The long-term vision includes public coordination, transparent governance, and a research-driven token economy.

Why It Matters

Bitcoin’s early era created a unique on-chain legacy worth studying and preserving.

SatoshiRelic exists to organize this effort in a public, structured, and transparent way.

The project stays focused on early Satoshi-era wallets and avoids expanding beyond its core mission.

Launch Model

The SatoshiRelic memecoin is Phase 1 of the project.

It serves as the public identity, information layer, and funding mechanism for the ecosystem.

Community support from the memecoin helps finance development, documentation, and early infrastructure.

Coordination Layer

SatoshiRelic will maintain a public ledger of completed research attempts.

This reduces duplicated work and helps distribute effort more evenly across the community.

The coordination layer is designed to make participation more efficient, visible, and organized.

Proof of Search

Verified research contributions are rewarded through a Proof of Search model.

This creates a measurable incentive for real work rather than passive speculation.

The token system is intended to align effort, transparency, and community participation.

Tokenomics

SatoshiRelic uses a transparent allocation model designed to separate liquidity, operations, founder incentives, community rewards, and treasury reserves.

Allocation Range Purpose
Liquidity Allocation 30–40% Initial market liquidity and launch stability.
Operations Allocation 20–30% First-phase project execution, including founder salary, AI tools, hosting, design, development, and launch expenses.
Founder Allocation 8–12% Long-term founder alignment, subject to vesting.
Community Allocation 20–30% Community rewards, growth incentives, airdrops, engagement, and future ecosystem participation.
Treasury Allocation 5–10% Long-term reserves, reporting, contingency, and future project expansion.

The Operations Allocation funds the project’s first phase and covers founder salary, AI tools, hosting, development, design, and launch execution.

The Founder Allocation is subject to vesting to ensure long-term alignment with the project.

Technology

The project may include blockchain-based coordination tools, smart contracts, treasury logic, public dashboards, and DAO governance modules.

These systems will be documented progressively as the technical architecture is finalized.

The website should present this as a living roadmap, not as a finished product.

Governance

A transparent DAO is planned to govern long-term rules and ecosystem decisions.

Smart contracts will manage defined on-chain actions and future conversion logic.

Governance is intended to be public, auditable, and rule-based.
